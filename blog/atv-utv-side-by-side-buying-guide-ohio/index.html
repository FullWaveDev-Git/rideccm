ATV, UTV & Side-by-Side Buying Guide for Ohio Riders

Everything you need to know before buying an off-road machine in Southwest Ohio — from sport quads to full-size UTVs.

Here's how to think through the decision.

Step 1: Understand the Categories

The terms ATV, UTV, and side-by-side get used interchangeably, but they describe genuinely different machines with different use cases. Get this right first and the rest of the decision gets a lot easier.

ATVs (All-Terrain Vehicles / Quads)

An ATV is a single-rider (sometimes two-rider) vehicle you straddle like a motorcycle, with handlebars for steering and four wheels for stability. They come in two broad flavors:

  • Sport ATVs — lightweight, high-revving machines built for motocross tracks and aggressive trail riding. Think KTM, Yamaha YFZ, Can-Am DS. These are performance-focused and not suited for work or hauling.
  • Utility ATVs — heavier, torquier machines with racks, towing hitches, and 4WD. Polaris Sportsman, Can-Am Outlander, Yamaha Grizzly. These are the workhorses — great for hunting, farm use, trail riding, and general purpose off-roading.

ATVs are more nimble and typically less expensive than side-by-sides. They're a great choice for single riders, tight trails, or anyone who wants to feel more connected to the terrain. Riders from Xenia, Springfield, and the rural corridors of Highland County tend to gravitate toward utility ATVs for their mix of trail capability and farm utility.

Side-by-Sides (SxS) / UTVs

Side-by-sides put two or more riders next to each other in a cab, with a steering wheel, and a roll cage. They range from small recreational machines all the way up to full-size 4-door UTVs with 1,000cc+ engines and more features than some pickup trucks. Key categories:

  • Recreational/Sport SxS — Can-Am Maverick, Polaris RZR series. Built for fun, speed, and aggressive off-road performance. Popular with younger riders and anyone who wants maximum thrills on the trail.
  • Family/Crossover SxS — Can-Am Defender, Polaris General, Yamaha Wolverine. The sweet spot for most buyers — capable off-road, comfortable for passengers, practical enough for light hauling. These are the most popular models we sell to Cincinnati and Dayton metro riders who want something the whole family can enjoy.
  • Work/Utility UTVs — Polaris Ranger, Can-Am Defender HD, Yamaha Viking. Serious payload, towing, and durability. These are what the farming and agricultural community around Washington Court House, Hillsboro, and rural Clinton County buy. Built to work as hard as you do.

Step 2: How Will You Actually Use It?

The biggest mistake buyers make is shopping for the machine they think looks coolest rather than the one that fits how they'll actually use it. Be honest with yourself about these questions before you start looking at specific models.

Who's Riding?

If it's just you, an ATV is often the better choice — more affordable, more nimble, easier to trailer and store. If you're regularly bringing a spouse, kids, or friends, a side-by-side makes more sense because it's safer and more comfortable for passengers. A lot of families from the Cincinnati suburbs — Loveland, Mason, Milford — make the drive to CCM specifically to get into a two- or four-seat side-by-side for weekend family trail rides.

What Terrain Are You Riding?

Ohio's off-road terrain varies a lot by region. If you're riding tight, wooded single-track trails in Tar Hollow State Forest south of Chillicothe or the Wayne National Forest corridors, a narrower ATV or compact SxS will serve you better than a full-size 64-inch-wide UTV that won't fit the trails. If you're mostly on open farm land, gravel roads, or wide ATV parks, a full-size side-by-side is no problem at all.

Riders from the Dayton metro heading to places like Paint Creek State Park or Caesar Creek off-road areas will find that mid-size side-by-sides in the 55-60 inch width range hit the sweet spot — wide enough to be stable and comfortable, narrow enough to handle most Ohio trails.

Work, Play, or Both?

If you need to haul hay, pull a sprayer, run fence lines, or do anything that involves regular work use, don't buy a sport machine. The Polaris Ranger and Can-Am Defender exist specifically for this purpose and they'll outlast a sport UTV in work conditions by years. If you just want to ride trails and have fun on weekends, the sport and recreational machines will be more rewarding. If you want both, the crossover/general-purpose models like the Polaris General and Can-Am Defender are genuinely excellent compromises.

Step 3: Set a Realistic Budget

Here's a rough breakdown of what to expect in 2026 pricing for new machines at CCM:

  • Entry-level ATVs — $5,000–$8,000. Youth models and smaller-displacement utility ATVs. Great starting point for new riders or farm utility use.
  • Mid-range utility ATVs — $8,000–$12,000. The sweet spot. Polaris Sportsman 450/570, Can-Am Outlander 570, Yamaha Grizzly 700. These cover 90% of what most Ohio riders need.
  • Performance ATVs — $10,000–$14,000. High-displacement sport and sport-utility machines.
  • Entry-level side-by-sides — $12,000–$16,000. Two-seat recreational and utility models.
  • Mid-range SxS — $16,000–$25,000. The largest part of our inventory. Covers the full range of Polaris General, Can-Am Defender, RZR Trail, and Yamaha Wolverine models.
  • Full-size performance SxS — $25,000–$35,000+. Can-Am Maverick X3, Polaris RZR Pro, and 4-door UTVs with full feature packages.

Don't forget to budget for accessories — a windshield, roof, sound system, light bar, and a quality set of tires for your terrain can easily add $2,000–$5,000 to the cost of a new side-by-side. We stock a full line of OEM accessories and aftermarket gear at CCM, so you can spec the machine out exactly the way you want it.

Step 4: New vs. Pre-Owned

We carry both new and pre-owned ATVs and side-by-sides, and the used market in 2026 is genuinely strong for buyers. Here's how to think about it:

Buy new if: You want the latest model, a full factory warranty, manufacturer financing specials, and the peace of mind of knowing the machine's complete history. Spring is one of the best times to buy new — manufacturer promotions are active and our inventory is freshest. Check our current promotions page for active deals.

Buy pre-owned if: You want more machine for your money, you're a first-time buyer who wants to learn on something without stressing about every scratch, or you're buying for a younger rider who may outgrow it in a couple years. Our pre-owned inventory is inspected by our factory-certified technicians before it hits the floor — we won't put a machine out there that we wouldn't ride ourselves.

Step 5: Think About Service and Parts Before You Buy

This is the step most buyers skip, and it's one of the most important. An ATV or side-by-side needs regular maintenance — oil changes, filter service, belt service on CVT-equipped machines, brake inspections, and seasonal tune-ups. When something breaks (and eventually something will), you want a dealer that can get you parts quickly and has technicians trained on your specific brand.

At CCM, we're authorized service dealers for every brand we sell. Our factory-certified technicians have completed manufacturer training on Polaris, Can-Am, Yamaha, and every other powersports brand we carry. We maintain a healthy OEM parts inventory in-house, which means faster turnarounds on repairs compared to dealers who have to order everything.

This matters a lot for riders who live farther out — Chillicothe, Washington Court House, Hillsboro, and rural Highland County riders especially. If your machine is down during hunting season or peak trail-riding weather, you want a service department that can turn it around in days, not weeks. That's what we aim to deliver.
